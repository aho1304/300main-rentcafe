How do you treat yourself?

How do you treat yourself? Whats your idea of a respite, a reward, a dip into relaxation, a retreat from reality? Would some say your tastes are, expensive? Perhaps exquisite cuisine and handcrafted libations are your foundation for R&R?

Rest assured, you beautifully cultured connoisseur of the fine life, for you are not alone! At 300 Main, our favorite way to clock out is with a glass in our hand, smile on our face, and an unbridled enthusiasm for the here and the now, stories told, meals shared, moments lived.

This bottle is our gift to you. Our frothy, natural sour culture infused, velvetly finished, peach-toned amuse bouche. Our 9.2% ABV sour tripel postcard from the other side, nudging you along to bend an elbow, put the phone down, have a compelling conversation, and treat yourself.

About the Artist
Danielle Fontaine Koslowsky is a Franco-Manitoban visual artist from Winnipeg, Canada working and living on Treaty 1 Territory. She serves on the MHC Gallery advisory committee, is a member of the Artist Mothers at MAWA, Creative Manitoba, and La Maison des Artistes Visuels Francophones. You can find her works in public and private collections across Canada & the US.

Danielles body of work explores the nebulous subject of the interior life. Her practice, rooted in the contemplative path and the study of the mystics, explores her own inner landscape; a fractal of the human experience. This work, like a mirror, reflects back to the self, its journey inwards towards the centre.

https://www.daniellefontainekoslowsky.com/

Nonsuch is a local Manitoban brewery focused on Belgian and European beers, situated in Winnipegs east Exchange District. All of the beers at Nonsuch are brewed using mineral water, pumped from an artesian well in Middlebro, Manitoba (this is in line with traditional Belgian brewing, and many breweries and distilleries in the UK are beside a river system for this reason). They are the only brewery using mineral water to brew instead of city water.

A tripel is a Belgian style of beer, a golden brew ranging from 8% to 9.5% ABV. Tripels are best warmed to at least 50 degrees. Never serve one fresh out of the fridge. They are very complex and serving them cold dulls your ability to taste all the goodness.

The 300 Main Sour Tripel beer comes in 750ml corked bottles to allow those who love cellar beers to enjoy it too. This is also thematic and connected to 300 Mains premium feel.
